Pricing framework · Steering discussion

Simplicity of the model vs better economic output

Two buckets for committee debate — bullets are starters, not decisions.

Simplicity of the model

  • One bill, one story — PDV, invoicing, and Nuvem Pago legible on a single narrative (avoid “surprise” lines).
  • Fewer dimensions — minimize the number of fee levers a store manager must track month to month.
  • CPT vs subscription — where to absorb complexity internally so the merchant sees a clean stack price.
  • Segment framing — hybrid vs standalone physical (per PMM) without multiplying SKUs.
  • Sales & support test — language that reps can quote in one breath; FAQs that match the statement.
  • Migration fairness — grandfathering and credits explained in one short policy, not case-by-case noise.

Better economic output

  • North star — optimize offline GMV and offline revenue through PDV-led in-store commerce (not ecommerce take rate).
  • Full-stack bundle — PoS SaaS + payments spread; avoid tuning either line in isolation.
  • NOTR / margin guardrails — consistency with 2026 BC and acquirer economics (Stone, dLocal, etc.).
  • Settlement & anticipation — effective MDR surfaced in CRM and quoting so credit economics are not opaque.
  • List anchors — non-promotional market bands; Upmarket vs SMB baselines for scenario planning.
  • Repricing loop — after material price moves, joint review of conversion and support signals by segment.
